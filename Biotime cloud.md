                <span class="section-caption center">REFLECTION</span>
                <h2 class="section-heading center">Key Learnings</h2>
                <p class="section-intro center">What global product leadership taught me about scale, culture, and technical debt.</p>
                
                <div class="learnings-grid">
                    <div class="learning-card">
                        <h4>Technical Debt Is a Market Expansion Tax</h4>
                        <p>We couldn't have served Nigeria's labor laws or China's data residency without first modernizing the API layer. The "tax" of legacy architecture was invisible until we tried to move fast globally.</p>
                        <span class="learning-caption">→ Validate: Pay down debt before scaling, or it compounds with every market</span>
                    </div>
                    <div class="learning-card">
                        <h4>Scrum Requires Cultural Translation</h4>
                        <p>My Shenzhen team preferred detailed written specs; my Bangalore team thrived with collaborative whiteboarding; my Mexico City stakeholders needed formal presentations. Same framework, different rituals.</p>
                        <span class="learning-caption">→ Validate: Methodology is constant; execution is contextual</span>
                    </div>
                    <div class="learning-card">
                        <h4>Executive Dashboards Are Products Too</h4>
                        <p>The Super Admin Dashboard started as a "nice-to-have reporting feature." It became the primary sales tool and the #1 driver of executive confidence. Internal tools deserve PM attention.</p>
                        <span class="learning-caption">→ Validate: B2B success often hinges on buyer (executive) experience, not just user experience</span>
                    </div>
                    <div class="learning-card">
                        <h4>Matrixed Organizations Need Product Diplomacy</h4>
                        <p>I had no direct reports, yet influenced 20+ engineers across 3 countries. Success required: clear documentation, data-backed decisions, visible wins for every team, and relentless communication.</p>
                        <span class="learning-caption">→ Validate: Influence without authority is the senior PM's core skill</span>
