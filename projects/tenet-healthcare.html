Case Study 08

Tenet Healthcare

Medical Billing Ecosystem — Simplifying Complexity for Healthcare Professionals

🏥 Medical Billing Platform 📉 5% User Confusion Reduction ⚡ 3% Productivity Improvement 🔗 50% Data Integration Accuracy
THE CHALLENGE

Medical billing is complex. The interface made it incomprehensible.

Tenet Healthcare operates one of the largest healthcare networks in the United States. Their medical billing ecosystem—spanning web and desktop applications—supported billions in revenue processing. But healthcare professionals struggled: confusing workflows, inconsistent terminology, and fragmented data integration created errors, delays, and frustration.

My challenge: Translate intricate medical billing processes into intuitive interfaces, improve data accuracy across systems, and enable healthcare staff to focus on patient care rather than paperwork navigation.

CONTRIBUTIONS

UX Simplification & Workflow Optimization

📉 UX Simplification

Translated intricate medical billing processes into user-friendly interfaces through qualitative research and iterative design. Successfully reduced user confusion by 5% by eliminating jargon, standardizing terminology, and creating clear task flows that matched mental models of healthcare professionals.

⚡ Workflow Optimization

Conducted qualitative research and user interviews with billing staff, nurses, and administrators to identify friction points. Refined medical billing flows through information architecture improvements, resulting in a 3% improvement in healthcare professional productivity.

⏱️ Task Efficiency

Designed intuitive information architectures that reduced user task completion time by 5%. Organized complex billing data into scannable, actionable views with progressive disclosure for detailed information.

🔗 Technical Integration

Partnered with backend database and marketing teams to achieve 50% accuracy improvement in complex data integration across the billing platform. Coordinated data mapping, validation rules, and synchronization workflows between multiple systems.

Research & Design Process

User Interviews

Conducted 20+ interviews with billing specialists, coding staff, and revenue cycle managers to understand pain points and workarounds.

Workflow Analysis

Mapped current-state billing processes to identify redundancy, error-prone steps, and compliance risks.

Iterative Prototyping

Created low-fidelity wireframes for rapid feedback, then high-fidelity prototypes for usability testing with actual billing staff.

Cross-Functional Coordination

Worked with backend engineering to ensure data models supported UX requirements, and with marketing to align terminology with user expectations.

REFLECTION

Key Learnings

Domain Complexity Requires User Partnership

Medical billing expertise lives with users, not designers. I learned to facilitate, not dictate—bringing billing staff into design decisions through participatory methods.

Accuracy Is Experience

In healthcare, data integration accuracy isn't backend plumbing—it's user trust. When billing codes sync correctly, staff trust the system and work faster.

Small Percentages, Large Impact

5% confusion reduction sounds modest. Across 10,000+ billing staff, that's hundreds of hours reclaimed for patient-focused work. Healthcare UX is high-leverage design.
